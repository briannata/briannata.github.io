By Sam Knapp

To-do list:
  • Study for summer courses
  • Take Astronomy midterm
  • Take Physics midterm
  • Present at Cisco Live

That was one intern’s to-do list the week of Cisco Live Las Vegas 2023.

How it started: The leaky water and overheating problem

Overheating and water leaks are two hazardous problems in computer labs.

Previously, the only way to make sure an HVAC system was running correctly was to have someone on-site to check it.

A superstar (Brianna Ta) takes the stage.

The pandemic made it extremely difficult to have a person at the lab for monitoring.

Enter Federal IoT Intern Brianna Ta to save the day.

Brianna and her teammates developed a custom IoT application server using open-source code, in-building sensors, and Webex alerts to remotely monitor HVAC systems in computer labs.

After her work started to gain some interest from customers and others at work, her manager Randy Benn submitted her IoT application server project for Cisco Live.

Two weeks after finishing finals at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Brianna and Randy walked onto the stage.

She’s not afraid to take on any kind of challenge. She’s not afraid to step up to speak at Cisco Live.

— Randy Benn

Brianna’s manager has nothing but praise for her.

Before her internship, Brianna was recognized as a Top 3 participant in the Cisco High program.

Randy was the site lead for Cisco High during Brianna's time and has had the opportunity to see her growth from high school student to kick-ass intern and Cisco Live speaker.

“Brianna has been a trailblazer since her time in high school. She had to be fearless then, and she is fearless in her work now,” said Randy.

Fearless (Brianna’s version)

The Federal IoT intern team keeps getting things done.

Nothing seems too scary or too challenging for Brianna — or if it does, she doesn’t let it show.

From being the only girl on her high school wrestling team to one of the youngest speakers at Cisco Live, Brianna continuously demonstrates that the next generation of Cisconians can accomplish anything.

What else is on her resume?

Lead Director for HackNC.

Don’t mind me, just hacking away.

Yes, that’s right. After participating in hackathons throughout high school and college, Brianna helps lead one of the largest hackathons in the Southeastern United States.

Her participation, leadership, and open-source code skills helped her develop the IoT application she shared at Cisco Live.

“Brianna has everything and more you could ask for in an intern,” said Randy. “She has the aptitude, initiative, organizational skills, technical skills, and discipline to get it done.”

Get to know Brianna

What hobbies do you enjoy?
Vietnamese Fan Dancing.

How many books have you read so far this year?
102. I read mostly fantasy, a little bit of science fiction, and murder mystery.

What’s the worst thing you’ve ever read?
A textbook.

Any other extracurriculars?
I’m the president of CS+ Social Good. It’s a club that helps social good organizations apply technology to their causes.

Who inspires you the most?
My parents. They work so hard.

My dad always tells me, “I came over here and didn’t know English, and I studied from the free books at the library.” Now he also works at Cisco. I have all the technology and opportunities I could ever ask for. They inspire me to work harder and provide for them in the future.

What’s your dream job?
I don’t really know, but definitely make cool things that help people.
